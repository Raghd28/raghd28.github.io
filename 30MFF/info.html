Facts: The Reality of Carbon Emissions

- The global average temperature has risen by over 1°C since pre-industrial times due to human activity.

- Transportation accounts for nearly 30% of global carbon emissions, with cars being one of the biggest contributors.

- If we reduce carbon emissions by 50% by 2030, we can slow down the most severe climate changes and protect our future.

Tips: Small Actions, Big Impact

- Walk or cycle instead of driving short distances, it’s good for the planet and your health.

- Reduce waste by choosing reusable products over single-use plastics.

- Support clean energy, opt for renewable power sources when possible.

- Eat more plant-based meals, livestock farming is a major carbon emitter.

- Be mindful of electricity use, turn off lights, unplug devices, and switch to energy-efficient appliances.

Significance: Why This Matters

- Reducing carbon emissions isn’t about sacrifice—it’s about creating a future where we can breathe cleaner air, enjoy thriving ecosystems, and live healthier lives.

- Every choice we make shapes the world we leave behind.

- A lower-carbon lifestyle isn’t just necessary—it’s the key to a better quality of life for everyone.
